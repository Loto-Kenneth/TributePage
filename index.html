Dr. Norman Borlaug

Dr. Norman Borlaug was a renowned agronomist who made significant contributions to agriculture and food security. He is often credited with starting the Green Revolution, which helped alleviate world hunger.

Achievements:

  • 1914: Birth and Early Life
    • March 25, 1914: Norman Ernest Borlaug is born in Cresco, Iowa, USA.
  • 1937-1938: Education and Research
    • Borlaug graduates from the University of Minnesota with a Bachelor of Science degree in forestry.
  • 1944-1960: Wheat Research in Mexico
    • 1944: Borlaug joins the Cooperative Wheat Research and Production Program in Mexico, working on wheat improvement.
    • 1953: He develops a semi-dwarf, high-yielding wheat variety called "Norin 10," which is resistant to lodging (falling over) and becomes a key component of the Green Revolution.
  • 1956: High-Yield Wheat Varieties
    • Borlaug and his team develop high-yield wheat varieties that significantly increase wheat production in Mexico and other parts of the world.
  • 1964: India and Pakistan
    • Borlaug's high-yield wheat varieties are introduced in India and Pakistan, leading to increased wheat production and helping to avert famine in these countries.
  • 1970: Nobel Peace Prize
    • Borlaug is awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for his contributions to the Green Revolution and for alleviating world hunger.
  • 1984: Medal of Freedom
    • He receives the Presidential Medal of Freedom, the highest civilian award in the United States.
  • 2006: Congressional Gold Medal
    • Borlaug is awarded the Congressional Gold Medal, another prestigious U.S. honor.
  • 2009: Passing
    • September 12, 2009: Dr. Norman Borlaug passes away in Dallas, Texas, leaving behind a legacy of agricultural innovation and humanitarian efforts.
